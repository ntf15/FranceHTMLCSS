            <h2>Introduction</h2>

            <p>France, officially known as the French Republic, is a country located in Western Europe. It is known for its rich history, art, culture, and cuisine. France is the largest country in the European Union by land area and the third-largest in Europe overall.</p>

        </section>


        <section>

            <h2>Geography</h2>

            <article>

                <h3>Location</h3>

                <p>France is situated in Western Europe, bordered by Belgium, Luxembourg, Germany, Switzerland, Italy, Monaco, Spain, Andorra, and the Mediterranean Sea.</p>

            </article>


            <article>

                <h3>Climate</h3>

                <p>France has a diverse climate, ranging from temperate in the north and west to Mediterranean in the south. The mountainous regions, such as the Alps and the Pyrenees, experience alpine climates.</p>

            </article>


            <article>

                <h3>Natural Hazards</h3>

                <p>France is prone to natural hazards such as flooding, avalanches in mountainous areas, and forest fires during the dry summer months.</p>

            </article>

        </section>

            <h2>People and Society</h2>

            <article>

                <h3>Population</h3>

                <p>France has a population of over 67 million people, making it the second-most populous country in the European Union.</p>

            </article>


            <article>

                <h3>Languages</h3>

                <p>French is the official language of France and is spoken by the majority of the population. Regional languages such as Breton, Corsican, Occitan, and Alsatian are also spoken in certain areas.</p>

            </article>


            <article>

                <h3>Religions</h3>

                <p>Christianity, particularly Catholicism, is the dominant religion in France. However, there is religious freedom, and other religions such as Islam, Judaism, and Buddhism are also practiced.</p>

            </article>

        </section>


        <section>

            <h2>Economy</h2>

            <article>

                <h3>Economic Overview</h3>

                <p>France has the fifth-largest economy in the world by nominal GDP and the second-largest in Europe. It is known for its diverse industries, including aerospace, automotive, luxury goods, and tourism.</p>

            </article>


            <article>

                <h3>Industries</h3>

                <p>Key industries in France include manufacturing, agriculture, tourism, and services. France is famous for its wine production, fashion, and high-speed trains.</p>

            </article>


            <article>

                <h3>Labor</h3>

                <p>France has a highly skilled labor force, with a strong emphasis on education and training. The labor market is regulated, with extensive labor laws governing wages, working hours, and employment contracts.</p>
